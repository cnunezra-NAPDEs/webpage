--- title: "Symplectic Hamiltonian HDG Simulations for Shallow Water Equations" collection: portfolio type: "Simulation" permalink: /portfolio/shallow-water-simulations date: 2026-04-12 --- This page presents numerical simulations for the shallow water equations obtained with **symplectic Hamiltonian HDG methods**. The goal is to illustrate how the method captures the wave dynamics while preserving the underlying Hamiltonian structure and providing a stable numerical approximation over time. In particular, these experiments highlight the role of the method in the **numerical conservation of energy** for the shallow water system. ## Experiment 1: Wavefront against a column This simulation shows the propagation of a wavefront interacting with a column obstacle. The experiment illustrates the ability of the symplectic Hamiltonian HDG formulation to resolve the dynamics while maintaining a physically meaningful energy behavior. The associated Hamiltonian is \[ \mathcal{H}[\mathbf{u}, w] = \frac{1}{2} \int_{\Omega} \left( |\operatorname{div}\mathbf{w}|^2 + \Phi |\mathbf{u}|^2 \right). \] ## Experiment 2: Bathymetry influence This simulation illustrates the influence of bathymetry on the wave propagation. In this case, the bottom topography modifies the flow structure, and the Hamiltonian formulation accounts for the corresponding forcing contribution.
